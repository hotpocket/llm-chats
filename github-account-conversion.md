Yes, you can convert a personal GitHub account to an organization, but it has **significant implications**:

### Key Consequences
- **You cannot log in** to the converted account—it becomes a non-login entity
- **You must create a new personal account** first to serve as the organization owner
- Your username becomes the organization name
- Repositories and teams transfer, but **Gists, personal settings, and profile data do not**
- Paid plans must be reconfigured under the new organization billing

### Quick Process
1. Create a new personal account (this will be your admin account)
2. Sign in to your original account
3. Go to **Settings → Account → Transform account**
4. Follow the wizard to transfer ownership to your new personal account
5. Sign in to your new account to manage the organization

### Recommendation
**Consider creating a new organization instead** (github.com/new) and manually transferring repositories. This preserves your personal account and avoids complications with Gists, stars, and other personal data.
